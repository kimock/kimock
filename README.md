## 1. Executive Summary

A KeyBERT text analysis of 153 safety violations recorded through March 23, 2026, shows that **Fall / Working at Height** issues ranked first at 48.4%, followed by **Lifting / Rigging** issues at 36.6%. **Housekeeping / Access Way** violations (28.8%) also continue to recur.

The number of "Uncompleted" cases stands at **104 (68.0%)** out of the total 153, indicating a low compliance rate that requires urgent strengthening of on-site safety management. Compared to the previous month (Feb 2026, 30 cases), violations have **surged 5.1 times**, necessitating a systematic safety management approach as site workload increases.

**Special Note for W5 (March 23):** All 9 recorded cases were from DSHI. This was the result of a concentrated inspection in the DSHI area on that day; therefore, W5 figures should be interpreted separately when comparing risks by vendor.

## 4. Top Issue Details

### 🔴 Rank 1 — Fall / Working at Height (74 cases, 48.4%)
Falling risks during high-altitude work account for nearly half of all issues. Recurring patterns include unfastened harnesses, unblocked openings, unsafe scaffolding, and working on narrow beams.
* **Keywords:** `scaffolding`, `lifeline`, `anchor bolt`, `fall arrestor`, `unsafe opening`
* **Uncompleted Cases:** 46 (62.2%)

### 🔴 Rank 2 — Lifting / Rigging (56 cases, 36.6%)
Primary causes include the absence of signalmen, failure to remove web sling rounds, and unauthorized entry into lifting zones. Often occurs simultaneously with Rank 1 issues.
* **Keywords:** `signalman`, `lifting area`, `unauthorized entry`, `web sling`
* **Uncompleted Cases:** 42 (75.0%)

### 🟡 Rank 3 — Housekeeping / Access Way (44 cases, 28.8%)
Scattered materials and blocked access ways lead to trips and collisions. This represents a recurring failure in basic on-site discipline.
* **Keywords:** `materials scattered`, `trip hazard`, `poor housekeeping`, `access way`
* **Uncompleted Cases:** 35 (79.5%)

## 6. Key Findings & Recommendations

1.  **Dominant Risks:** Fall and Lifting issues account for **85.0%** of cases (including overlaps). **Recommendation:** Strengthen TBM before high-altitude/lifting work and mandate two-person verification of harness fastening.
2.  **Low Compliance:** The overall **uncompleted rate of 68.0%** suggests poor follow-up after the March surge. **Recommendation:** Share daily uncompleted status and enforce mandatory deadlines for corrective actions.
3.  **High Equipment Risk:** **95.2% of equipment-related issues remain unaddressed**. **Recommendation:** Mandate the submission of safety checklists before using Scissor Lifts, JCBs, or Boom Lifts.
4.  **Growth Trend:** Violations increased **5.1 times** in March. **Recommendation:** Review the need for additional safety personnel and increased inspection frequency proportional to the site activity level.
